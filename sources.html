Sources


There are various Hemophilia treatment centers, organizations, or communities that are present around the globe. You can check some if you wish to participate in local communities or donate for a global cause. You can check the list below:


Local

Hemophilia Advocates Philippines or HAP. Started on September 17, 2016 and officially registered as a non-profit organization two months later on November 15, 2016. A national organization of persons with hemophilia and related bleeding disorders, family members and advocates. An organization registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission as non-stock, non-profit organization. The main objective is to provide immediate assistance to our members who are experiencing life-threatening bleeds. Visit their site here.

Hemophilia Association of the Philippines for Love and Service or HAPLOS. Established to help people with hemophilia in the Philippines and their families attain a better quality of life and become productive members of society. HAPLOS programs are aimed at empowering and developing Persons with Hemophilia (PWH) to become physically active, mentally sharp, socially involved, emotionally healthy, spiritually strong. Visit their Facebook page here.

International

World Federation of Hemophilia or WFH. a non-profit organization dedicated to improving and sustaining care for people with inherited bleeding disorders around the world. Work in partnership with healthcare providers (HCPs), governments, and our global network of national member organizations (NMOs) in 147 countries. We provide our NMOs and healthcare providers with the knowledge and tools they need to identify, support and treat people living with bleeding disorders in their communities, while promoting global advocacy and collaboration to achieve our common goals. Visit their site here.

National Hemophilia Foundation or NHF. Dedicated to finding cures for inheritable blood disorders and to addressing and preventing the complications of these disorders through research, education, and advocacy enabling people and families to thrive. Founded in 1948 by Robert and Betty Jane Henry, parents of a young boy with hemophilia. When their son, Lee, was born, the only treatment for hemophilia was blood transfusions. Visit their site here.

Haemophilia Foundation Australia or NFA. The national peak body that represents people with haemophilia, von Willebrand disease and other bleeding disorders, and their families. HFA is a National Member Organisation of the World Federation of Hemophilia, and participates in activities to improve access to treatment and care to people with bleeding disorders around the world. Visit their site here.
